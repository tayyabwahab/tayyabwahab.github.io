Automated White Matter Segmentation in MR Images Using Residual UNet

International Conference on Advancements in Computational Sciences â€¢ Vol. 78, pp. 123-145

Authors: Y Ali, MA Iftikhar, Q Abbas, T Wahab

Abstract: Vascular changes in small vessels can be observed through Flair MR images called white matter hyperintensities (WMHs). WMHs are associated with cerebral small vessel disease, aging, dementia, and stroke. Quantification of WHMs is important for diagnosis, prognosis, monitoring of patients, and research studies. Manual segmentation of WHMs is a time-consuming task and is subjective to the observer. That's why we need an automated segmentation method. WHMs' segmentation is a challenging task due to their heterogeneous characteristics and coexistence with other similar-appearing structures. We proposed an architecture that uses residual blocks in a U-Net-based network to segment the WHMs from T1-weighted, Flair images of brains. In this study, we used the MICCIA White Matter Hyperintensities Challenge 2017 Dataset to train and test the proposed model. We evaluated the proposed method using the standard MWM challenge in 2017 evaluation measures and achieved better results than the state-of-the-art technique.

Brain Tumor Segmentation Using Deep Learning Technique: 2D U-Net Model Variant for Tumor Segmentation

Recent Advancements in Smart Remote Patient Monitoring, Wearable Devices, and Diagnostics Systems â€¢ pp. 54-67

Authors: MH Khan, T Wahab

Abstract: Cancer is one of the most lethal diseases in the world. A brain tumor is a form of cancer that develops in the brain's glial cells. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is a prominent imaging tool for detecting brain tumors. It includes four different modalities that neurologists use to determine the location and kind of tumor. The suggested approach uses a 2D U-Net model to separate the brain tumors sub regions. To prevent excessive preprocessing and GPU utilization, the authors utilize the patching approach to partition the picture slices into distinct patches in this study. Second, they leverage the squeeze and excitation blocks to more effectively map low-level features to high-level features than a basic U-Net. The suggested technique yields DICE scores of 0.85, 0.87, and 0.90 for the three tumor categories of enhancing tumor, whole tumor, and tumor core, respectively. The results outperform the most recent approaches, including the major papers from the Brats 2019 competition.

Study on Efficient Image Restoration using Reference Image

Advanced Web Development Techniques â€¢ Volume 19, pp. 645-650

Authors: Intaek Kim, Tayyab Wahab Awan

Abstract: Image restoration is required when the image is blurred due to out of focus or motion during the image acquisition. This type of image restoration is known as ill-posed inverse problem because the estimate of an original image should be derived from only one blurred image. This paper introduces a reference image to facilitate the restoration process. The experimental result shows that computation time is significantly reduced, compared with other methods. The proposed method obtains the estimate of the kernel used in blurring processing. New cost function is defined to update both the image and the kernel alternately. In the last stage, Wiener filter produces the estimate of an original image using the kernel and the reference image.

Tracking occluded objects using kalman filter and color information

International Journal of Computer Theory and Engineering â€¢ Volume 6 â€¢ pp. 438-442

Authors: Malik M Khan, Tayyab W Awan, Intaek Kim, Youngsung Soh

Abstract: Robust visual tracking is imperative to track multiple occluded objects. Kalman filter and color information tracking algorithms are implemented independently in most of the current research. The proposed method combines extended Kalman filter with past and color information for tracking multiple objects under high occlusion. The proposed method is robust to background modeling technique. Object detection is done using spatio-temporal Gaussian mixture model (STGMM). Tracking consists of two steps: partially occluded object tracking and highly occluded object tracking. Tracking partially occluded objects, extended Kalman filter is exploited with past information of object, whereas for highly occluded object tracking, color information and size attributes are used. The system was tested in real world application and successful results were obtained.

Background subtraction-based multiple object tracking using particle filter

IEEE IWSSIP 2014 Proceedings â€¢ pp. 71-74

Authors: Intaek Kim, Tayyab Wahab Awan, Youngsung Soh

Abstract: In surveillance system, many vision based methods are utilized for multiple objects. Some of the methods used for tracking include background modeling, Kalman filter, particle filter etc. The proposed method combines both the background modeling and particle filter to track multiple objects. This method consists of two steps: background subtraction and application of particle filter. Background subtraction restricts the area of search so that the particle filter performs efficiently. Different colored particles are displayed on the object for tracking. After applying the proposed method on a video having multiple objects, successful results were obtained.

Multiple Viewpoint Generation with a Single Dome Camera and Inter-viewpoint Object Tracking

Advanced Materials and Information Technology Processing â€¢ Vol. 87 â€¢ pp. 425

Authors: Yongsuk Hae, Aamer Mehmood, Hadi Ashraf, Malik M Khan, Mudasar Qadir, Tayyab Wahab, Intaek Kim, Youngsung Soh

Abstract: For security purposes, it is necessary to monitor several places at the same time with CCTV cameras and track objects such as human and vehicles across viewpoints. Most of the current implementation uses multiple cameras to generate multiple views and tracks objects within each viewpoint. The proposed system adopts a single dome camera, generates multiple views using camera preset functionality, and tracks objects across multiple viewpoints. For tracking, object detection should be done first. We use the background model suggested by Soh et al. [1] to segment foreground from background. Tracking consists of two steps: intra-viewpoint tracking and inter-viewpoint tracking. For intraviewpoint tracking we exploit extended Kalman filter (EKF) [2], whereas for inter-viewpoint tracking, attribute matching is used. We tested the system in real world application and obtained successful results.

Multi-target tracking using color information

International Journal of Computer and Communication Engineering â€¢ Vol. 3, pp. 11

Authors: Intaek Kim, Malik Muhammad Khan, Tayyab Wahab Awan, Youngsung Soh

Abstract: For security purposes, it is prerequisite to track multiple targets efficiently. Most of the current implementation uses Kalman filter and color information independently. The proposed method combines extended Kalman filter and color information for tracking multiple objects under high occlusion. For tracking, the first thing done is the object detection. The background model used to segment foreground from background is spatio-temporal Gaussian mixture model (STGMM). Tracking consists of two steps: independent object tracking and occluded object tracking. For independent object tracking we exploit extended Kalman filter, whereas for occluded object tracking, color information attribute is used. The system was tested in real world application and successful results were obtained.

Object Tracking under Heavy Occlusion based on Extended Kalman Filter, Particle Filter, and Color Matching

International Journal of Engineering and Innovative Technology (IJEIT) â€¢ Vol. 4

Authors: Tayyab Wahab Awan, Ahmed Mahdi Abed, Intaek Kim, Hyuk Soo Jang, Minho Shin

Abstract: Object tracking is useful in areas such as surveillance, access control, military applications, and health care. Objects may be human, vehicle, and other entities. A huge number of approaches have been proposed in this field. Different methods assume different environments and adopt various techniques ranging from deterministic to probabilistic approaches. Excellent surveys can be found in [1]. In this paper, we proposed a method for tracking objects by exploiting probabilistic power of track filters including extended Kalman filter and particle filter in the presence of heavy occlusion. We also use color matching to improve on tracking results. We applied the method to real world videos and obtained successful results.
